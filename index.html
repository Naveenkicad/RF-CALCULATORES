Formula Reference
ΔH = (Air_AGL + Air_AMSL) − (GND_AGL + GND_AMSL) — net vertical separation (m)
Worst-case Horiz = Mission Radius (m) — UAV at edge of orbit
Slant Range = √( HorizDist² + ΔH² ) — 3D LOS distance (m → shown in km)
Elevation Angle = arctan( ΔH / HorizDist ) — look-up angle from GND antenna to Air unit (°)
Beam Spread = 2 × SlantRange × tan( BW / 2 ) — physical width of beam at slant distance (m)
Coverage Span = 2 × ΔH × tan( BW / 2 ) — nadir ground footprint of Air antenna beam cone (m)
LOS Status — IN BEAM: Mission Radius ≤ Coverage Span / 2 · EDGE: within 20% · OUT: beyond beam

❓ Help & Guide
Quick start guide to the calculator's main tabs and workflow.
1. Enter your link parameters — use the Link Budget, FSPL, SNR & Mod, Losses, Doppler and GEO / AMSL tabs to enter frequency, power, antenna gains, distance, and losses. Inputs auto-recalculate as you type or move sliders.

2. Check the Overview tab — gives a one-glance dashboard summary of your current link margin and key results.

3. Tracker tab — computes required Azimuth/Elevation pointing angles and shows a live animated dish/UAV diagram.

4. Trade-offs tab — compares range vs. data rate and other design trade-offs.

5. 💡 Suggest tab — auto-suggests configuration changes to improve your link margin.

6. 🔒 Premium tab — unlocks advanced tools: 3D Map Simulation, Terrain & Fresnel, Beam Coverage, Interference, Antenna Datasheet parser, and Antenna/Mission Coverage geometry. See the "Get Premium" tab above for access.
Generate a shareable report of your current results.
1. Click the ⬇ Export ▾ button in the top-right header.
2. Choose a format:
  • 📊 Excel Report (.xlsx) — 12 live-formula sheets; edit the yellow cells and everything recalculates (budgets, FSPL table, modulation, range sweep, trade-offs, geometry & more).
  • 📝 Word Report (.docx) — a full written report with all sections, tables, Doppler, FSPL sweep, trade-off sweep and notes.
  • 📄 PDF Report (.pdf) — a full report including tables and all 18 charts at full size.
3. You may be prompted to give the export a title — type one or click Skip.
4. The file downloads automatically to your device.
